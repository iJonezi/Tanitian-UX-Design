Welcome, to Paradise!

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific. While the island has an area of less than 500 square miles, the terrain is varied and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small but safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small, active volcano. Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000. Until a recent increase in tourism, most the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

No matter what your interests, you can find something to do on Taniti. The island is home to a number of unique attractions, including the world's largest collection of Polynesian artifacts, a botanical garden with over 1,000 species of tropical plants, and a variety of hiking trails that wind through the island's lush rainforests. Why wait? Book your trip to Taniti today and experience all that this beautiful island has to offer!
